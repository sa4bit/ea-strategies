MACD Strategy EA

üìò Strategy Description

This EA uses the MACD Histogram across 3 selectable timeframes ‚Äî HTF, ITF, and ETF ‚Äî to determine market momentum and signal direction.

You can configure MACD values for each timeframe independently. A trade is triggered only when the MACD Histogram conditions align across the selected timeframes.

‚úÖ Entry and Exit Behavior:

Entry Timeframe: Choose HTF / ITF / ETF (Selectable)

Exit Timeframe: Choose HTF / ITF / ETF (Selectable)

‚úÖ Entry Conditions:

  • Buy: MACD Histogram is above the zero line on the selected Entry Timeframe (e.g., M15), and (if selected) MACD is also above zero on higher filters like H1 (ITF) and H4 (HTF). The trade closes when the MACD histogram drops below zero on the selected Exit Timeframe.
  • Sell: MACD Histogram is below the zero line on the selected Entry Timeframe (e.g., M15), and (if selected) MACD is also below zero on higher filters like H1 (ITF) and H4 (HTF). The trade closes when the MACD histogram rises above zero on the selected Exit Timeframe.

If Exit Timeframe is set to H4, the trade will not close on M15 signal, but instead wait for H4 condition to break.

‚úÖ Optional Filter:

  • ADX Filter: You may enable ADX to confirm trending conditions and avoid sideways markets, applied on any selected timeframe. If enabled, the EA verifies:

    • ADX value > 25, and
    • +DI > -DI (for Buy) or -DI > +DI (for Sell)

    Just like MACD, the ADX filter strengthens directional confirmation by filtering out weak or sideways markets. If ADX is enabled on HTF (e.g., H4), it works as a trend-strength filter on that timeframe.

üîÅ Manual Trade Handling

If a trade is manually closed, the EA will not immediately re-open the trade under the same signal. It will wait for a new valid signal to avoid noise and false repetition.

üîç Multi-Timeframe Logic:

HTF, ITF, and ETF act as optional filters to confirm trend alignment. Entry and Exit Timeframe selections determine where the actual trade signal and closure are evaluated.

A trade enters when the Entry Timeframe condition is satisfied (e.g., MACD above/below zero), and exits when the Exit Timeframe condition reverses ‚Äî provided all enabled filters remain aligned.
