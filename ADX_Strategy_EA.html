ADX Strategy EA

üìò Strategy Description

This EA uses the ADX indicator across multiple timeframes (HTF, ITF, ETF) to confirm the strength and direction of a trend. You can configure Entry and Exit Timeframes, while using other timeframes as filters for trend alignment.

An optional EMA/SMA filter can be enabled to help confirm the directional bias alongside ADX.

‚úÖ Entry and Exit Behavior:

Entry Timeframe: HTF / ITF / ETF (Selectable)

Exit Timeframe: HTF / ITF / ETF (Selectable)

‚úÖ Entry Conditions:

  • Buy: ADX > 25 and +DI > -DI on the selected Entry Timeframe, and (if selected) ADX confirms trend strength and direction on HTF (e.g., H4) and ITF (e.g., H1)..

    • The trade closes when +DI < -DI or ADX drops below 25 on the selected Exit Timeframe (e.g., M15).

  • Sell: ADX > 25 and -DI > +DI on the selected Entry Timeframe, and (if selected) ADX confirms bearish strength on HTF and ITF.

If Exit Timeframe is set to H4, the trade will not close on M15 signal, but instead wait for H4 condition to break.

‚úÖ Optional Filter:

  • SMA Filter: You may enable EMA on any timeframe (HTF, ITF and ETF) for directional confirmation. If enabled, the EA checks whether:

    • Price is above EMA (for Buy) on selected timeframes, and
    • Price is below EMA (for Sell) on selected timeframes

    The EMA filter helps ensure trades are only taken in the direction of the broader trend. For example, if EMA is enabled on HTF (e.g., H4), it acts as a trend direction filter for that timeframe.

    üîÅ Manual Trade Handling

    If a trade is manually closed, the EA will not immediately re-open the trade under the same signal. It will wait for a new valid signal to avoid noise and false repetition.
